The plastic pollution problems facing the world can seem overwhelming, but there are reasons to be positive, too.

On this page we shout out people and teams who are taking on the issues and making a positive difference.

The Ocean Cleanup is taking on ocean pollution

A non-profit foundation created in 2013, The Ocean Cleanup is designing and deploying innovative solutions to rid the world's oceans of plastic.

They're aiming to stem the flow of plastics into the ocean from rivers and to remove 90% of the plastic floating on the ocean's surface by 2040.

Source: The Ocean Cleanup

Fungus and bacteria could help deal with hard-to-recycle plastics

A bio-manufacturing company called Biohm is studying fungi that can break down PET plastics, which are often single-use and hard to recycle. The company discovered a strain of fungus that could consume plastic by accident when it escaped its jar by eating its way through the plastic seal.

Some scientists believe that it could be possible to develop fungi and bacteria that not only consume plastics, but turn them into useful products like food or antibiotics.

Source: BBC

Soluble and edible seaweed could replace plastic packaging

An Indonesian company called Evoware is developing an alternative to plastic packaging made from seaweed. The product they're developing dissolves quickly in water and can even be eaten.

Plastic packaging accounts for 141 million tonnes of plastic waste per year, so creating sustainable alternatives could prevent a huge amount of waste.

Source: Evoware
